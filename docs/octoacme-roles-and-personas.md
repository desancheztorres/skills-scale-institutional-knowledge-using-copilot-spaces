# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Owner**: Seeks clarification on user stories and acceptance criteria
- **Product Manager**: Provides technical feasibility input on proposed features
- **Project Manager**: Reports progress, identifies blockers and technical risks
- **Business Analyst**: Clarifies technical constraints and validates requirement feasibility
- **QA Lead**: Collaborates on test automation, fixes defects, and reviews quality metrics

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Owner**: Provides strategic vision and validates backlog alignment with business goals
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on roadmap delivery, timeline expectations, and resource planning
- **Business Analyst**: Collaborates on understanding market needs and translating to requirements
- **Developers**: Discusses trade-offs, technical constraints, and solution approaches
- **Stakeholders**: Gathers input, validates product direction, and communicates outcomes

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Owner**: Coordinates on sprint planning, scope changes, and timeline impacts
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on roadmap delivery, dependencies, and resource allocation
- **Developers**: Tracks progress, removes blockers, and facilitates team ceremonies
- **Business Analyst**: Monitors requirements status and identifies scope risks
- **QA Lead**: Reviews testing progress, quality metrics, and release readiness
- **Stakeholders**: Provides status updates, manages expectations, and escalates issues

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between stakeholders and development teams by gathering, analyzing, and documenting business requirements. They ensure that technical solutions align with business needs and user expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create detailed functional specifications and user stories
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Maintain traceability between requirements and deliverables

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and testable requirements
- Minimize rework by catching requirement gaps early
- Bridge communication between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements review sessions with Product Owner and stakeholders
- Clarification meetings with developers on acceptance criteria
- Status updates on requirements analysis and documentation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Owner**: Collaborates to refine product vision into detailed requirements
- **Product Manager**: Works together to understand market needs and business objectives
- **Developers**: Translates business needs into technical requirements and answers questions
- **QA Lead**: Ensures requirements are testable and provides test scenarios
- **Project Manager**: Reports on requirements status and identifies scope changes
- **Stakeholders**: Regular engagement to validate requirements and gather feedback

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owner maximizes the value of the product by managing the product backlog, defining user stories, and making prioritization decisions. They serve as the primary decision-maker for product features and scope.

### Responsibilities
- Own and prioritize the product backlog
- Write and refine user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Make trade-off decisions on scope and features
- Accept or reject completed work based on acceptance criteria
- Ensure the team builds the right features at the right time
- Maintain stakeholder alignment on product direction

### Goals
- Maximize return on investment (ROI) for development efforts
- Ensure team works on highest-value items
- Maintain a healthy, well-groomed backlog

### Typical Communication
- Daily availability for team questions and clarifications
- Backlog refinement sessions with team
- Sprint planning and review participation
- Stakeholder demos and feedback sessions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Business Analyst**: Collaborates on detailed requirements and user story elaboration
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on strategic direction and validates backlog priorities
- **Developers**: Clarifies acceptance criteria and answers implementation questions
- **QA Lead**: Reviews acceptance criteria and participates in acceptance testing
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on timeline, scope changes, and resource needs
- **Stakeholders**: Demonstrates progress and gathers feedback

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Lead ensures quality throughout the development lifecycle by defining testing strategies, managing test activities, and advocating for quality standards. They lead the quality assurance team and coordinate testing efforts.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test strategy and test plans
- Lead test planning, estimation, and execution
- Ensure adequate test coverage across functional and non-functional requirements
- Coordinate different testing types (unit, integration, system, acceptance)
- Track and report on quality metrics and defect trends
- Establish and enforce quality gates and standards
- Mentor QA team members

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high product quality and reliability
- Optimize test coverage and efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Daily sync with development team on quality status
- Test plan reviews with Product Owner and Business Analyst
- Quality metrics reporting to Project Manager and stakeholders
- Defect triage meetings with developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Business Analyst**: Reviews requirements for testability and creates test scenarios
- **Product Owner**: Validates acceptance criteria and participates in acceptance testing
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test automation, reviews code quality, and triages defects
- **Project Manager**: Reports quality status, testing progress, and risk assessment
- **Stakeholders**: Provides quality confidence reports and demo participation

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are individuals or groups with interest in or influence over the project. They may include executives, end users, customers, business unit leaders, or other teams that depend on or are affected by project outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business context, constraints, and success criteria
- Review and approve key project decisions and deliverables
- Supply resources, budget, or organizational support as needed
- Participate in requirements validation and feedback sessions
- Communicate organizational priorities and constraints
- Accept final deliverables and sign-off on project completion

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers expected business value
- Protect organizational interests and investments
- Influence project direction to meet business needs

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or milestone-based status reviews
- Approval gates for major decisions or scope changes
- Feedback sessions on prototypes or releases
- Project initiation and closure meetings

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Business Analyst**: Provides requirements input and validates documented needs
- **Product Owner**: Communicates priorities and provides feedback on product direction
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on strategic objectives and market needs
- **Project Manager**: Receives status updates and makes escalation decisions
- **QA Lead**: Reviews quality reports and participates in acceptance demonstrations
